BUBBLE CPAP

Bubble CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) is a type of respiratory support that delivers a constant flow of air and oxygen to the lungs of newborns who are having difficulty breathing. It is most commonly used in premature infants or those with respiratory distress syndrome.

The bubble CPAP machine consists of a nasal interface, tubing, and a water chamber that creates positive pressure in the airways. The water chamber serves as a resistance to the airflow, maintaining a constant pressure that helps keep the baby's lungs expanded. The bubbles produced in the water chamber create a gentle oscillation, which helps to keep the airways open and improves oxygenation.

Bubble CPAP is a non-invasive method of respiratory support that is effective in reducing the need for mechanical ventilation and the associated risks and complications. It is easy to use, and the water level in the chamber can be easily adjusted to vary the pressure. Bubble CPAP can be used in the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) or during transport of the neonate to another facility.

While bubble CPAP is generally a safe and effective treatment, the medical team will closely monitor the baby's breathing, oxygen saturation, and other vital signs to ensure that the treatment is working properly. In some cases, the baby may need additional support, such as surfactant therapy or mechanical ventilation.
