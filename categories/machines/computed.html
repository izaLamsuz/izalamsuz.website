CT (COMPUTED TOMOGRAPHY) SCANNER

CT SCANNER:combines X-rays images taken from different angles to create cross-sectional images of the body. CT scans are valuable for diagnosing conditions such as trauma, tumors, and internal bleeding.

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN MRI (MAGNETIC RESONANCE IMAGING) AND CT(COMPUTED TOMOGRAPHY) SCANNER
MRI (MAGNETIC RESONANCE IMAGING) CT(COMPUTED TOMOGRAPHY) SCANNER
CT scans use X-rays to create images of the area that was scanned MRIs use strong magnetic fields to take pictures of the inside of the body
CT scans are usually the first choice for imaging MRIs are useful for certain diseases that a CT scan cannot detect
CT scans are generally goood for larger areas. MRI scan produces a better overall image of the tissue under examination.
CT scans use radiation (X-rays) MRIs do not
MRIs provides more detailed information about the inner organs such as the the brain, skeletal system, rerproductive system, and other organs systems than is provided by a CT scan
