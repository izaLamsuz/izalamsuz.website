  • Normal pulse oximeter readings usually range between 95% and 100%. It measures the oxygen saturation in your blood.     • HOW DOES PULSE OXIMETER WORK?

  • The measurement are obtained by simply shining two wavelength of light (one is visible red beam, the other is invisible infrared beam) to reach the light detector.
  • Finger is placed in between the light source and the light detector, the light will now have to pass through the finger to reach the detector. Part of the light will be absorbed by the finger and the part not absorbed reaches the light detector.
  • The amount of light that is absorbed by the finger depends on many physical properties and these properties are used by the pulse oximeter to calculate the oxygen saturation.

    • The amount of light absorbed depends on the following:

  • concentration of the light absorbing substance.
  • length of the light path in the absorbing substance
  • oxyhemoglobin and deoxyhemoglobin absorbs red and infrared light differently.

    How it Works?

    Pulse oximetry works by shining a small beam of light through the fingertip or earlobe. The sensor measures the amount of light that passes through the blood in the finger or earlobe. Oxygenated blood absorbs light differently than deoxygenated blood, which allows the device to calculate the amount of oxygen in the blood.     Benefits of Pulse Oximetry

    Some of the benefits of Pulse Oximeters include:

    • Quick and easy to use, with results in seconds.
    • Non-invasive and painless.
    • Helps in the immediate detection of hypoxemia and other respiratory conditions such as COPD or COVID19.
    • Helps in monitoring and managing respiratory conditions like asthma and COPD.
    • Can be used at home, in clinics, hospitals, or during exercise to monitor oxygen levels during workouts.

    Types of Pulse Oximeters

    There are mainly two types of pulse oximeters.

    • Fingertip Pulse Oximeters: These pulse oximeters clip onto the fingertip and are the most commonly used type of pulse oximeter. It is portable and easy to use, making it popular among athletes, pilots, and people with respiratory conditions.
    • Wrist-Worn Pulse Oximeters: These pulse oximeters come in the form of a wristwatch, and they are worn on the wrist like a regular wristwatch. These devices provide continuous monitoring that can pick up changes in blood oxygen levels over time during sleep or other activities.

    Conclusion

    Pulse oximeters are valuable medical devices that can be used at home or in hospitals and clinics to measure the amount of oxygen in a person's blood. It is non-invasive, painless, and can provide quick results. They can be used easily and effectively to monitor respiratory conditions, such as COPD and asthma, and identify oxygen levels in the blood. The device comes in different types, such as fingertips or wrist-worn pulse oximeters, making it convenient and easy to use in different care settings.
